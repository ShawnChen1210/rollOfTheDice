Bugs & Cheats in Gambling

 

Discover the notorious bugs and cheats that have shaped the history of gambling, from classic casino stories to modern digital glitches. Come with us as we lift the veil on the captivating stories that have placed themselves into history of gambling. From the crafty card players of old to the modern-day digital daredevils, these stories showcase individuals who dared to challenge the mighty casino.

 

Richard Marcus

 

Richard Marcus, a notorious cheater in gambling history, used the "Savannah" method to remove chips from losing bets. He placed a small denomination chip on the roulette table, swapping the higher value chip for another $5 chip. If he won, he celebrated, causing the dealer to mistakenly assume he had staked two $5 chips. Marcus won thousands on winning bets and lost only a few dollars on losing bets.

 

Tommy Glenn Carmichael

 

Carmichael, a successful slot machine cheater, developed a metal device that triggered payouts in 1980. As casinos updated their slots, he moved to smaller gambling establishments in Las Vegas. He developed the monkey paw or slider, which released coins from machines. He continued cheating until the advent of electronic slot games. He was arrested multiple times and sentenced to more prison time.

 

Ida Summers

 

Ida Summers, known as the Vegas Vixen in the 1960s, used her beauty and charm to distract pit bosses and dealers in Las Vegas casinos. She worked blackjack tables using hand-mucking techniques and cold decks, or coolers, to ensure the house lost. Despite her success, Summers was eventually caught and arrested by gaming officials in Vegas and the FBI. Her appearance and charm may have had an effect in court, as she received probation rather than a custodial sentence, suggesting her looks and charm may have affected her case.

 

Monique Laurent

 

In 1973, French woman Monique Laurent and her team at Deauville Casino won over $1 million from roulette tables using sophisticated equipment and a rogue dealer. Despite the casino's attempts to find the problem, Laurent's beauty led to her arrest, and the casino owner began to suspect her of wrongdoing.

 

Louis Colavecchio

 

Louis Colavecchio, a notorious counterfeiter, created thousands of slot machine coins for American casinos. After being arrested in 1998, he resumed his activities in 2006, but was arrested again in a few months.

 

Dennis Nikrasch

 

Dennis McAndrew, also known as Nikrasch, is a notorious slot machine cheater who made over $15 million using sophisticated methods. He was arrested in 1998 for cheating mechanical slot machines. Despite the computerization of slot machines, Nikrasch continued to develop new methods, using a crew of people recruited through his contacts. It is unknown how many times he broke into machines before being caught.

 

Bill Brennan

 

Brennan, a cashier at Stardust Hotel and Casino in Las Vegas, allegedly heistked half a million dollars in cash and chips in 1992. He left the casino and has never been seen since. Rumors suggest he was killed by an accomplice who didn't want to split the proceeds, but no evidence has been found. The common belief is that Brennan is still alive, enjoying his ill-gotten gains, somewhere in the world.

 

Ron Harris and Reid McNeal

 

Reid McNeal hit a $100,000 Keno jackpot in 1995, but computer programmer Ron Harris discovered he had modified slot machine software to pay out large wins. McNeal and his accomplice cheated thousands of dollars and developed a computer program to predict Keno numbers. Harris was sentenced to seven years.
